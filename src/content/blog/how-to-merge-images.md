## What Is Image Merging?

Image merging means combining two or more separate images into a single image file. You might need this for:

- **Before/After comparisons** — Show a product before and after editing
- **Social media collages** — Combine 2-4 photos into one post
- **Product showcases** — Display multiple product angles in one image
- **Screenshot documentation** — Stack multiple screenshots into a single document
- **Portfolio layouts** — Arrange work samples side by side

## Tips for Best Results

### Match Image Dimensions

When merging **horizontally**, best results come from images with the same **height**. When merging **vertically**, match the **widths**. If your images have different sizes, use [FilesOk's resize tool](https://filesok.com/okimg/resize-image) first to standardize dimensions, then merge.

### Compress Large Files First

FilesOk's merge tool handles images in your browser's RAM. Very large images (10MB+ each) may slow processing on older devices. Try compressing them first with [FilesOk's image compressor](https://filesok.com/okimg/compress-image), then merge.

### Gap Color is White

The gap between images uses a white background. If your images have dark backgrounds, set the gap to 0px to avoid a visible white divider.

### Output is JPEG

FilesOk exports the merged result as JPEG at 90% quality — excellent quality with minimal compression. Process once and download to avoid re-compression artifacts.
