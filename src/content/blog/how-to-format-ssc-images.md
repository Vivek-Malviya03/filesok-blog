## The Official SSC Photo & Signature Requirements

Under the updated SSC portal guidelines, the application process handles photographs and signatures very differently. While your **photograph must be captured live** using a webcam or mobile browser module during the application, your **signature must be prepared and uploaded as a scanned image file**. 

| Document Type | Required File Format | Permitted File Size | Standard Dimensions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Live Photograph** | JPEG / JPG | Real-time Capture | Camera Frame (Face Centered) |
| **Scanned Signature** | JPEG / JPG | **10 KB to 20 KB** | Approx. 4.0 cm × 2.0 cm (~236 × 79 px) |

> **Crucial Rules to Remember:**
> - **No pre-existing photos:** Uploading old gallery photos or taking a picture of an existing physical photo will result in an automatic rejection.
> - **No CAPS in signatures:** Writing your full name in capital block letters instead of a natural signature will disqualify your application.

## Why Is Your File Getting Rejected?

If the SSC portal rejects your signature upload, it is almost always caused by one of these three technical mistakes:

- **Wrong Format:** Many modern smartphones save cropped images as PNG or WebP files. The SSC system strictly locks uploads to **JPEG/JPG** formats only.
- **Incorrect File Size:** If your file is 9.9 KB or 20.1 KB, it sits outside the absolute **10 KB – 20 KB** boundaries and will fail.
- **Stretched Aspect Ratio:** If your scanned crop is a perfect square, forcing it into the wide signature frame will distort the image, making it unreadable.

## Best Practices for a Successful Live Photo Capture

Because you cannot upload a pre-saved passport picture, you need to maximize your chances of approval during the live webcam or mobile phone capture phase on the official portal:

- **Find a Plain Wall:** You must stand or sit against a solid, light-colored or white background. Textures, busy patterns, curtains, or visible room clutter will cause an automated rejection.
- **Remove Eye Gear and Headwear:** You must remove all spectacles, sunglasses, caps, or masks—even if you wear prescription glasses on a daily basis. Your eyes and facial features must be fully uncovered.
- **Level the Camera:** Keep your smartphone or laptop webcam directly at eye level. Look straight ahead into the lens with a neutral expression. Ensure your face fills the rectangular zone completely without being too close or cut off.
