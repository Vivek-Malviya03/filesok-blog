Every social media platform has different image dimension requirements. Post a photo to Instagram with wrong dimensions and it gets cropped or letterboxed. Upload to LinkedIn with blurry quality and your professional image (literally) suffers.

The frustration? Each platform has different requirements:
- Instagram: 1080×1080 for posts, 1080×1350 for stories
- Twitter: 1200×675 for headers, 1024×512 for images
- LinkedIn: 1200×627 for posts, different for articles
- Facebook: 1200×628 for posts, 16:9 for videos

Resizing each image manually in Photoshop takes 5 minutes per image. Do this for 20 posts across 4 platforms? You're spending hours on a task that should take minutes.

This guide shows you the exact dimensions for every major platform, plus how to batch resize images so you can do 50 images in less time than it takes to make coffee.

## Social Media Image Dimensions: The Complete Reference

### Instagram

Instagram is obsessed with specific aspect ratios. Get them wrong and your beautiful photo shows with white bars.

| Content Type | Dimensions | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Feed Post | 1080×1080 | 1:1 (Square) | Most forgiving; all images work |
| Feed Post | 1080×1350 | 4:5 (Vertical) | Recommended; takes more feed space |
| Stories | 1080×1920 | 9:16 (Vertical) | Full screen, temporary |
| Reels | 1080×1920 | 9:16 (Vertical) | Video format, but same dimensions |
| IGTV | 1080×1920 | 9:16 (Vertical) | Original orientation |

**Pro tip:** Instagram intelligently crops images, so don't put critical content on edges. Keep important details in the center 70% of your frame.

### Twitter/X

Twitter's image requirements are smaller than Instagram but still matter. Feed images scroll past quickly, so dimension choice affects how much viewers see.

| Content Type | Dimensions | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Post Image | 1024×512 | 2:1 (Landscape) | Default, shows full in feed |
| Post Image | 1200×675 | 16:9 (Landscape) | Also common, flexible |
| Header/Banner | 1500×500 | 3:1 (Landscape) | Profile header |
| Profile Photo | 400×400 | 1:1 (Square) | Small but visible |

**Pro tip:** Twitter recommends 16:9 (1200×675) for best compatibility with most devices and feed layouts.

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn cares about professional appearance. Blurry or cropped images damage credibility.

| Content Type | Dimensions | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Post Image | 1200×627 | 16:9 (Landscape) | Feeds and shares |
| Article Cover | 1200×500 | 12:5 (Landscape) | Blog posts, documents |
| Company Page Banner | 1500×500 | 3:1 (Landscape) | Profile header |
| Profile Photo | 400×400 | 1:1 (Square) | Professional headshots |

**Pro tip:** LinkedIn prioritizes text-to-image ratio. Avoid images that are 100% photo with no text overlay—LinkedIn's algorithm favors educational/shareable content.

### Facebook

Facebook is surprisingly flexible with aspect ratios, but there are still optimal dimensions.

| Content Type | Dimensions | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Feed Post | 1200×628 | 16:9 (Landscape) | Standard post |
| Story | 1080×1920 | 9:16 (Vertical) | Full-screen vertical |
| Reel | 1080×1920 | 9:16 (Vertical) | Video format |
| Page Banner | 820×312 | 16:9 (Landscape) | Company/profile header |

**Pro tip:** Facebook compresses images more aggressively than Instagram. Use slightly higher quality when preparing, since Facebook will reduce it further.

### Pinterest

Pinterest is vertical and image-focused. This is where tall, narrow pins dominate.

| Content Type | Dimensions | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Pin | 1000×1500 | 2:3 (Vertical) | Standard pin |
| Pin | 1000×1200 | 5:6 (Vertical) | Square-ish, still vertical |
| Profile Banner | 1500×500 | 3:1 (Landscape) | Header |

**Pro tip:** Taller pins (2:3) perform better on Pinterest. They take up more vertical space in feeds, increasing visibility.

## Common Resizing Mistakes & How to Fix Them

### ❌ Stretching Images to Fit Dimensions

**Problem:** Resizing 1920×1080 directly to 1080×1080 stretches the image (squashes people's faces, distorts products)

**Solution:** Resize with "Lock aspect ratio" on, then crop excess. Or use "Fit" mode which adds borders.

### ❌ Losing Quality When Enlarging

**Problem:** Taking a small 400×400 image and enlarging to 1200×1200 creates pixelated mess

**Solution:** Always resize down, never up significantly. If needed, use upscaling tools (AI upscalers like Upscayl exist), but start with high-res originals.

### ❌ Ignoring Platform Compression

**Problem:** Instagram/Facebook compress images after you upload. If you over-compress locally, result is blurry.

**Solution:** Compress less aggressively for social media (JPEG 80-85 quality). Platforms will compress further, and you want buffer room.

### ❌ Using Wrong Aspect Ratios

**Problem:** Uploading 16:9 image to Instagram (which expects 1:1 or 4:5), resulting in letterboxing

**Solution:** Check platform requirements *before* resizing. Our table above has all major platforms.

## Pro Tips for Social Media Success

### 1. **Create Dimension Templates**

Save your target dimensions as presets:
- "Instagram Feed" = 1080×1080
- "LinkedIn Post" = 1200×627
- "Twitter Header" = 1200×675

Reuse these instead of entering dimensions each time.

### 2. **Optimize for Mobile First**

60%+ of social media viewing happens on mobile. Ensure:
- Text is readable (min 12pt)
- Important content isn't on edges (may crop on mobile)
- Colors have good contrast (easier to read on small screens)

### 3. **Test Before Publishing**

Upload a test image to each platform. See how it displays on mobile before publishing your 30-image campaign.

### 4. **Use Consistent Branding**

Resize all images to same dimensions = consistent feed aesthetic. Inconsistent dimensions = chaotic-looking profile. Consistency = professionalism.

### 5. **Schedule Posts for Consistency**

Use scheduling tools (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite) to post regularly. Regular posting + consistent image dimensions = higher engagement and algorithm favorability.
